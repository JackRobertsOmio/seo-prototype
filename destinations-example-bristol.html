Bristol
A vibrant, creative city famous as Banksy's hometown and the birthplace of iconic street art. Bristol blends cutting-edge culture with Georgian architecture, a thriving harbourside, and an independent spirit. From world-class museums to a legendary music scene, this is where tradition meets innovation.

Clifton Suspension Bridge
Isambard Kingdom Brunel's masterpiece spanning the Avon Gorge. Walk across for panoramic views, visit the exhibition centre for engineering history.
Admission: £8 | Open daily 9am-6pm
SS Great Britain
Brunel's revolutionary iron ship, now a museum. Explore the historic vessel, engine rooms, and interactive exhibits about Victorian innovation.
Admission: £18.95 | Open daily 10am-5:30pm
Bristol Museum & Art Gallery
World-class collection spanning Egyptian mummies, natural history, and contemporary art. Free entry to permanent collections.
Free admission | Open daily 10am-6pm
M Shed
Waterfront museum in a Georgian warehouse. Explore Bristol's history from slavery to street art, with interactive displays.
Free admission | Open daily 10am-5pm
Banksy Trail
Self-guided or guided tours of famous street art including the Girl with the Pierced Eardrum, Mild Mild West, and ever-changing murals.
Free (self-guided) | Guided tours from £12
Cabot Tower
16th-century tower in Brandon Hill Park offering 360-degree views over Bristol. Climb the 98 steps for stunning vistas.
Free admission | Open daily 10am-4pm
Street Art Walking Tour
Guided exploration of Bristol's famous murals and graffiti art. Learn the stories behind iconic pieces and emerging artists in Stokes Croft.
£12-15 per person | 2-3 hours
Harbour Boat Trip
Scenic cruise on the Avon harbour. See the city from the water, pass historic vessels, and enjoy commentary on local landmarks.
£8-10 per person | 30-45 minutes
Climbing at The Climbing Academy
Indoor climbing gym with routes for all levels. State-of-the-art facility with bouldering walls, ropes, and instruction available.
Day pass: £14 | Open daily
Live Music
Catch live gigs at iconic venues. Thekla (world music on a cargo ship), The Fleece (indie and alternative), and many intimate clubs.
Tickets: £8-30 depending on artist

General Safety
Bristol is generally safe for tourists. Central areas like Harbourside, city centre, and main shopping streets are well-populated and secure.
Low crime rate in tourist zones
Petty Crime
Watch for pickpockets in crowded areas and on public transport. Keep valuables secure, avoid leaving belongings unattended, don't flash expensive items.
Standard urban precautions apply
Night Safety
The city centre is safe at night with good street lighting and police presence. Avoid isolated areas. Use licensed taxis or Uber; avoid unmarked cabs.
Stay aware, travel in groups when possible
Areas to Avoid
Some outer neighbourhoods are less touristy but generally safe. Stick to main streets and well-lit areas. Avoid very late-night walks alone.
Neighbourhoods are generally friendly

Contactless Payment
Everywhere accepts contactless cards and mobile payments (Apple Pay, Google Pay). Minimum spend limits rarely enforced. Preferred method across Bristol.
Standard payment method
ATMs
ATMs widely available throughout Bristol. Withdraw GBP cash at any Barclays, HSBC, or Link ATM. No fees at major banks.
Multiple locations, fee-free
Currency Exchange
Exchange currency at airport, train station, or banks. Avoid tourist exchange booths with poor rates. Use ATMs for better exchange rates.
ATMs offer best rates
Tipping
10% at restaurants for good service (optional). 10-15% for excellent service. Tipping is appreciated but not mandatory in the UK. Tip in cash or add to card payment.
Cultural norm but optional
Credit Cards
Visa and Mastercard widely accepted. American Express less common but increasingly accepted. Notify your bank of travel to avoid card blocks.
Essential to carry

Public Buses
Comprehensive network operated by First Bus and other providers. Reliable, frequent service across the city. Pay contactless or with a Day Rider ticket (£5 for 24-hour travel).
£2.10 per journey | Day pass: £5
Harbour Ferry Boats
Historic floating harbour system. Water ferries connect Bathurst Basin to SS Great Britain and beyond. Scenic alternative to buses, fun for sightseeing.
£2.50-4 per journey | Scenic route
Walking
Bristol's city centre is compact and walkable. Most attractions within 20-30 minutes on foot. Harbourside, Stokes Croft, and Clifton are excellent walking areas.
Free, healthy, most rewarding
Cycling
Bike-friendly city with cycle paths and quiet routes. Cycle hire available (Voi and Citybike schemes). Great for exploring neighbourhoods and riverside paths.
Voi scooters: £0.50 + £0.20/min
Taxis & Ride-Sharing
Licensed black cabs and Uber available. Reliable and affordable, especially at night. Book through apps for safety and traceable journeys.
£5-15 typical journey
Train Connections
Bristol Temple Meads is the main railway station. Direct trains to Bath (12 min), London Paddington (1.5 hrs), Cardiff (50 min), and many UK cities.
Central location, modern station
Tips for Getting Around
Get a contactless card or use your phone for payment. Download the First Bus app for route planning. Most journeys within the city take 15-25 minutes. Plan travel for off-peak to save time.
Preparation makes travel easy
Tip: Bristol is hilly; wear comfortable shoes and allow extra time when walking. Spring weather changes quickly—check forecasts for each day and bring layers.
