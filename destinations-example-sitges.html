Sitges

A charming coastal town just 35 minutes from Barcelona, Sitges captivates visitors with its pristine beaches, colorful modernista architecture, vibrant LGBTQ+ friendly culture, and legendary Carnival celebrations. Perfect for a beach escape or cultural weekend.

🏖️
Beautiful Beaches
Golden sandy beaches with calm waters, lifeguards, and beach bars perfect for swimming and relaxation.
🏛️
Old Town
Narrow winding streets lined with galleries, boutiques, and cafés in the charming Casco Antiguo historic district.
🎨
Maricel Museum
Stunning modernista palace housing an impressive collection of Catalan and Mediterranean art and sculpture.
🎭
Carnival & Festivals
Vibrant Carnival in February and International Film Festival in October bring energy and celebration year-round.
Church of Sant Bartomeu i Santa Tecla
Iconic white modernista church with twin spires overlooking the seafront. Built in 1884, it's Sitges' most recognizable landmark and offers beautiful views from the plaza.
Maricel Museum
Former modernista palace with an exceptional art collection including sculptures, paintings, and decorative arts. The building itself is an architectural masterpiece with terraced gardens.
€6 admission • Wed-Sun 10am-5pm
Cau Ferrat Museum
Charming bohemian-style house museum with eclectic art collections, antique furniture, and memorabilia from the artistic community. Home to works by El Greco and contemporary artists.
€5 admission • Thu-Sun 10am-5pm
Palau de Maricel
Stunning modernista palace built in 1910 with intricate architecture, ornate interiors, and a magnificent garden overlooking the sea. Used for exhibitions and cultural events.
Casco Antiguo (Old Town)
Narrow winding streets lined with local galleries, artisan shops, cafés, and restaurants. Perfect for wandering and discovering hidden gems, street art, and local culture.
Tip: Visit museums early in the day to avoid crowds, and explore the old town during evening paseo for authentic local atmosphere.
Beach-hopping
Explore Sitges' multiple beaches including Playa Principal for families, Playa de la Ribera for a more local vibe, and coves like Playa del Xarco. Each offers unique character and facilities.
Free • Year-round
Wine tasting in Penedès
Visit local wineries in the nearby Penedès wine region (20 minutes by car) for tastings of Catalan wine, Spanish sparkling Cava, and local specialties. Many offer tours and food pairings.
€20-50 per person • Book ahead
Seafront promenade walk
Stroll the beautiful paseo marítimo along the waterfront with views of beaches, chiringuitos (beach bars), and the church. Perfect for sunset walks or morning jogs.
Free • Best at sunset
Carnival celebration
Experience Sitges' legendary Carnival in February with parades, costumes, live music, dancing, and vibrant street parties. One of Spain's most famous and lively carnivals with huge LGBTQ+ presence.
February • Book accommodation early
Film festival
Visit during the International Film Festival (October) featuring avant-garde, fantasy, and experimental cinema. Screenings at local venues, outdoor projections, and red carpet events.
October • Festival passes available
Sailing & water sports
Rent sailboats, kayaks, or paddleboards from local operators. Take a boat trip along the coast or join a sailing excursion for a different perspective of Sitges and nearby coves.
€30-80 • Book at beach clubs
Tip: Book Carnival and Film Festival accommodations 2-3 months in advance. Spring (March-May) offers perfect weather for all outdoor activities.
Barcelona
Spain's vibrant second city just 35 minutes away. Explore Gaudí's Sagrada Familia, Park Güell, Gothic Quarter, La Rambla, museums, restaurants, and nightlife. Full day or overnight trip recommended.
35 min • €3-8 by Rodalies train
Tarragona
Historic Mediterranean city with Roman ruins, an impressive amphitheater overlooking the sea, medieval old town, cathedral, and local beaches. Rich cultural heritage and excellent seafood restaurants.
45 min • €4-10 by train
Vilanova i la Geltrú
Quieter coastal town with beautiful beaches, a charming maritime museum, and relaxed atmosphere. Great for a peaceful beach day or exploring a less touristy Catalan seaside destination.
10 min • €1.50-3 by train
Penedès wine region
Famous wine-producing area with numerous wineries offering tastings, tours, and food pairings. Sample local cava, white wines, and reds while learning about Catalan winemaking tradition.
20-30 min by car • Guided tours available
Montserrat
Dramatic mountain monastery with stunning rock formations, spiritual significance, hiking trails, and panoramic views. Visit the basilica, hike to viewpoints, or take the cable car for mountain adventures.
1 hour • Combined train+cable car tickets available
Tip: Buy a T-10 ticket (10 journeys) for regional trains to save money. Rodalies trains run frequently between Sitges and Barcelona (€3-8).
Seafood restaurants
Sitges is famous for fresh seafood. Try grilled fish, gambas al ajillo (garlic shrimp), mejillones (mussels), and caldereta de marisco (seafood stew). Most waterfront restaurants serve excellent fresh catches daily.
€15-35 per main course
Paella and arròs dishes
Traditional paella valenciana and seafood paella (paella de marisco) are local specialties. Many restaurants offer beach paella experiences for lunch overlooking the Mediterranean Sea.
€14-28 per person • Order ahead
Xató salad (local specialty)
A traditional Catalan salad unique to the Sitges area made with endive, salt cod, tuna, anchovy, and a special romesco sauce. A must-try local dish found in many restaurants and tapas bars.
€8-12 per serving
Carrer del Primer de Maig restaurant strip
This charming street in the old town is lined with restaurants, tapas bars, and cafés. Great for exploring diverse dining options, from traditional Catalan to international cuisine. Excellent for evening paseo and dining.
Various price ranges • Casual atmosphere
Catalan cuisine specialties
Don't miss pan con tomate (bread with tomato), calcots (roasted leeks), botifarra (Catalan sausage), escalivada (roasted vegetables), and esqueixada (salt cod salad). Perfect with local wines and cava.
€5-15 per dish
Tip: Lunch is typically 1-3pm, dinner after 8pm. Many restaurants close between 3-7pm. Weekday lunches offer better value than weekends.
Accommodation costs
Sitges is slightly cheaper than Barcelona but pricier than inland towns. Budget hotels €50-80/night, mid-range €80-150/night, boutique hotels €150-300/night. Prices surge during Carnival and film festival.
Meal costs
Budget menu del día (lunch special) €10-15, casual meal €12-20, moderate restaurant €20-35, upscale dining €40+. Seafood dishes and waterfront restaurants are pricier. Tapas and casual dining offer good value.
Transport costs
T-10 regional train ticket €11.35 (10 journeys in Barcelona metro area). Single journey to Barcelona €3-8. Buses within Sitges free to low cost. Most of town is walkable.
Museum and attractions
Museum entries €4-6 each. Beaches are free. Activities like wine tastings €20-50 per person. Book combination tickets for better value. Many cultural events and activities are free or low-cost.
Overall budget estimate
Budget traveler: €60-90/day. Mid-range: €100-150/day. Splurge: €200+/day. Sitges is moderately priced with good value outside peak season. Spring and autumn offer the best value with pleasant weather.
Money-saving tip: Visit during off-season (April-May, September-October) for lower prices and fewer crowds. Avoid Carnival and festival weekends for better deals.
Casco Antiguo (Old Town)
The historic heart of Sitges with narrow winding streets, local galleries, artisan shops, boutiques, and traditional restaurants. Home to the Church of Sant Bartomeu i Santa Tecla. Perfect for exploring local culture and wandering.
Seafront (Paseo Marítimo)
The waterfront promenade with beaches, beach clubs, restaurants, bars, and hotels. Main tourist area with water sports facilities, lifeguards, and the best views of the Mediterranean. Lively day and night atmosphere.
Terramar
Upscale residential neighborhood with beautiful villas, parks, and quieter beaches. Home to cultural institutions and the Palau de Maricel. More relaxed than the center, perfect for peaceful beach access and scenic walks.
Vinyet
Charming beach area south of the old town with a quieter vibe, local chiringuitos, and a more authentic Catalan feel. Great for experiencing local beach culture away from the main tourist crowds while staying close to action.
Tip: Base yourself in the Old Town for walkable access to restaurants, galleries, and nightlife. Stay near Seafront for beach convenience. Choose Terramar or Vinyet for a quieter, more local experience.
Boutique hotels
Charming small hotels with character, often in modernista buildings or beachfront locations. Many feature rooftop terraces, personalized service, and local art. Prices €120-250/night for quality experience.
€120-250/night
Beachfront rentals
Apartments and villas with sea views, terraces, and kitchen facilities. Perfect for families or longer stays. Available through Airbnb, Vrbo, and local property managers. Prices vary widely based on season.
€80-300/night • Book early for summer
Budget accommodations
Hostels, basic hotels, and guesthouses offer budget options €40-70/night. Located inland from beach, but walkable to everything. Good for solo travelers and those watching their budget.
€40-70/night
Seasonal pricing
Peak season (July-August, Carnival February, Film Festival October): prices 2-3x higher. Spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) offer best value with pleasant weather. Winter is cheapest but fewer activities.
Tip: Book 2-3 months ahead for Carnival and Festival. Consider nearby towns (Vilanova, Castelldefels) for cheaper alternatives with easy train access. Apartment rentals offer better value for groups or longer stays.
General safety
Sitges is a very safe small coastal town with low crime rates. Tourist areas are well-policed and secure. Standard precautions (avoiding isolated areas at night, watching valuables) apply as in any tourist destination.
Beach safety
Designated swimming areas with lifeguards during summer months (flags indicate safe conditions). Check wave conditions and currents. Swim in marked areas only. Most beaches are monitored and family-friendly.
Water conditions
Mediterranean water is generally calm, but winter and spring can have stronger currents and larger waves. Water temperature: 14-18°C in spring, 20-22°C in summer. Respect warning flags and lifeguard instructions.
Valuables and pickpocketing
Watch belongings in crowded areas and on beaches. Use hotel safes for documents and extra cash. Avoid displaying expensive jewelry or electronics. Street crime exists but is rare; exercise normal urban precautions.
Nighttime safety
Sitges is safe at night with good street lighting in main areas. Stick to main streets, the seafront, and popular bars. The LGBTQ+ community is well-integrated and welcomed. Avoid walking alone in very dark side streets late at night.
Tip: In an emergency, call 112 (EU emergency number). Keep a copy of your passport separate from the original. Travel insurance is recommended. Tourist police are helpful with reports needed for insurance claims.

Beach essentials
Swimsuit, beach towel, flip-flops, beach bag. Beaches are rocky in some areas, so water shoes or sandals are helpful. Bring a change of clothes for after-beach meals at seafront restaurants.
Sun protection
High SPF sunscreen (reapply frequently), sunglasses, wide-brimmed hat, and sun shirt. Mediterranean sun is intense even in spring. A lightweight cover-up protects from sun and wind.
Spring layering
Days are mild (15-20°C) but mornings/evenings can be cool. Pack a light sweater, thin jacket, or cardigan. Comfortable walking shoes for exploring the old town. Light scarf is versatile for layering and style.
Casual dining wear
Dress code is casual except for upscale restaurants. Pack nice casual clothes for evening paseo and dining. Comfortable walking shoes, sandals for casual restaurants, and modest beachwear cover most situations.
Optional items
Portable charger for phones, small day backpack, reusable water bottle (refill at cafés), light waterproof jacket for spring showers, comfortable clothing for wine tastings in Penedès region.
Tip: Leave space in luggage for shopping as Sitges has excellent boutique galleries and craft shops. Quick-dry clothing is practical for beach days. The town is very walkable, so comfortable shoes are essential.
Currency
Spain uses the Euro (EUR, symbol €). 1 Euro = 100 cents. Coins: 1, 2, 5, 10, 20, 50 cents; €1, 2. Banknotes: €5, 10, 20, 50, 100, 200. Check current exchange rates before traveling.
Cards and ATMs
Visa, Mastercard, and American Express widely accepted. ATMs are abundant in Sitges, Barcelona, and most towns. Withdraw cash in EUR to avoid poor exchange rates. No fees at most Spanish ATMs for major banks.
Cash vs. cards
Most establishments accept cards, including small shops and restaurants. Some traditional establishments and markets prefer cash. Carry a mix of both. Contactless payments are standard and widely accepted.
Tipping culture
Tipping is NOT expected in Spain. Service is included in restaurant bills. Rounding up to nearest 5-10 EUR or leaving 5-10% at upscale restaurants is appreciated but optional. Tip bar staff with coins or small change.

Tip: Notify your bank of travel dates to avoid card blocks. Don't exchange money at airports (poor rates). Use ATMs in central areas for security. Keep some emergency cash separate from main wallet.
Walking
Sitges is very walkable and pedestrian-friendly. The entire town center is accessible on foot. Main beaches, old town, and attractions are within 15-20 minutes walking. Wear comfortable shoes for uneven old town streets.
Rodalies trains (Cercanías)
Regional train network connects Sitges to Barcelona (35 min, €3-8), Vilanova (10 min), and Tarragona (45 min). Frequent departures (every 15-30 min). Buy individual tickets or T-10 card (€11.35 for 10 journeys). Very convenient and economical.
Buses
Local buses connect different areas of Sitges. Most transport needs are walkable. Long-distance buses (to Barcelona, Tarragona) available from main station. Playa line buses useful for reaching different beaches.
Taxis and rideshare
Taxis available at station and main plazas. Uber and Bolt operate in the area. Useful for reaching Penedès wineries or late-night returns. More expensive than trains but convenient with luggage.
Bicycle rental
Local bike rental shops offer daily rentals (€15-25/day). Flat terrain perfect for cycling. Bike-friendly paths along seafront and connecting to nearby towns. Great for exploring at your own pace and reaching quieter beaches.
Tip: Get a T-10 ticket at any metro/train station for flexibility on regional trains. Walking is the best way to experience Sitges. Rent a bike for reaching nearby beaches. Trains are the most economical way to Barcelona.
