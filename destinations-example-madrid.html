Madrid
Step off the train at Atocha and you're already in the heart of it — Retiro Park is a 5-minute walk, the Prado 10. Madrid is a city that rewards wandering: tapas in La Latina, sunset from Templo de Debod, churros at 3am at Chocolatería San Ginés. Spring is perfect — warm days, terrace season, and the San Isidro festival in May fills the streets with music and food.

Museo del Prado
Spain's national art museum. Velázquez's Las Meninas, Goya's dark paintings, and Bosch's Garden of Earthly Delights. Allow 2–3 hours minimum.
Free Mon–Sat 6–8pm, Sun 5–7pm · Full ticket €15
Reina Sofía
Home to Picasso's Guernica — the most powerful anti-war painting ever made. The 20th-century collection is world-class.
Free Mon, Wed–Sat 7–9pm, Sun 12:30–2:30pm · Full ticket €12
Royal Palace (Palacio Real)
Europe's largest royal palace with 3,418 rooms. The Throne Room and the Royal Armoury are standout highlights.
Free for EU citizens last 2 hours · Full ticket €12
Retiro Park
125 hectares in the city center. Row a boat on the lake, visit the Crystal Palace, or just people-watch along the promenades.
Free · Open 6am–midnight
Templo de Debod
An actual Egyptian temple, gifted to Spain in 1968. The best sunset spot in Madrid — locals gather here every evening.
Free · Closed Mondays
Flamenco show
Corral de la Morería and Cardamomo are the top tablaos. Intimate venues where you're a metre from the dancers. Book ahead.
From €25–45 with a drink
Football at Santiago Bernabéu
Real Madrid's newly renovated stadium seats 80,000. Match tickets start around €50; stadium tours available on non-match days.
Tour from €25 · Match tickets from €50

Guided walking tours through La Latina and Lavapiés neighborhoods. A great way to discover hidden local bars you'd never find on your own.
From €35–60 per person, food included
Spanish cooking class
Learn to make paella, tortilla española, or gazpacho. Most classes include a market visit to pick up ingredients.
From €50–80 per person
Spring tip: Toledo and Segovia are ideal in April–May before summer heat. Book AVE tickets a few days ahead for the best prices.
Toledo — The "City of Three Cultures"
Medieval walled city with a cathedral, Alcázar, and El Greco paintings around every corner. Compact enough to explore in half a day.
🚆 30 min by AVE from Atocha · From €13 return
Segovia — Roman Aqueduct & Roast Suckling Pig
The 2,000-year-old aqueduct is jaw-dropping. Walk to the Alcázar (which inspired Disney's castle), then eat cochinillo at a local mesón.
🚆 27 min by AVE from Chamartín · From €12 return
Ávila — Medieval Walls You Can Walk On
The best-preserved medieval walls in Europe. Walk the full circuit for panoramic views. Quieter and more atmospheric than Toledo.
🚆 1h 30m by Media Distancia · From €16 return
El Escorial — Philip II's Royal Monastery
A massive Renaissance monastery-palace in the foothills of the Sierra de Guadarrama. The library alone is worth the trip.
🚆 1h by Cercanías from Atocha · From €7 return
Aranjuez — Royal Gardens by the River
The Versailles of Spain. Stunning formal gardens, a royal palace, and strawberry fields. The historic Strawberry Train runs in spring.
🚆 45 min by Cercanías from Atocha · From €6 return
Must-try dishes
Cocido madrileño (hearty chickpea stew, served in courses) · Bocadillo de calamares (fried squid sandwich — a Madrid thing) · Churros con chocolate at San Ginés (open since 1894, busiest at 3am) · Patatas bravas · Tortilla española
La Latina — Tapas capital
Calle de la Cava Baja is wall-to-wall tapas bars. Try Casa Lucas for patatas bravas or Juana La Loca for their famous tortilla pintxo.
Mercado de San Miguel
Gourmet tapas market right off Plaza Mayor. Pricier than neighborhood bars but great for a first-night sampler of Spanish food.
Mercado de San Fernando (Lavapiés)
The local alternative — multicultural food stalls, natural wines, and a crowd that's 90% madrileños. Thursday and Friday evenings are best.
Eating
A casual meal with a drink: €8–12. A sit-down dinner: €25–45. Menú del día (set lunch) is the best deal at €12–16 for 3 courses + drink.
Transport
Metro single: €1.50. 10-trip pass: €12.20. Tourist day pass from €8.40.
Sightseeing
Major museums offer free evening hours. Retiro Park, Templo de Debod, and many churches are always free. Budget €30–50/day for a comfortable visit with attractions.
Money-saving tip: The menú del día is available at most restaurants Mon–Fri lunchtime. It's how locals eat well on a budget — 3 courses, bread, drink, and sometimes dessert for €12–16.
La Latina
The tapas heart of Madrid. Go on Sunday for El Rastro flea market, then spend the afternoon bar-hopping along Cava Baja and Cava Alta.
Malasaña
Madrid's creative and vintage quarter. Plaza del Dos de Mayo for weekend vermú, independent shops, street art, and late-night bars.
Chueca
Madrid's LGBTQ+ neighborhood — stylish boutiques, rooftop bars, and the liveliest terrace scene in the city. Especially vibrant during Pride in July.
Lavapiés
Madrid's most multicultural barrio. Indian, Senegalese, and Chinese restaurants next to traditional Spanish bars. The Tabacalera art center is worth a look.
Salamanca
Upscale Madrid. Designer shopping on Calle Serrano, fine dining, and beautiful 19th-century architecture. A different side of the city.
Budget — Hostels & budget hotels
Madrid has excellent hostels in the center. The Hat, Way Hostel, and Cats Hostel are highly rated and right by Sol/La Latina. Dorm beds from €18–30/night.
Mid-range — Boutique hotels
Gran Vía and Malasaña have great 3–4 star options. Expect €80–140/night. Look for hotels near Sol or Atocha for easy access to trains and sights.
Upscale — Luxury & design hotels
The Salamanca district and Paseo del Prado area have Madrid's best luxury hotels, including the new Four Seasons and the Mandarin Oriental Ritz. From €250/night.
Best area to stay: Sol/La Latina for nightlife and tapas. Near Atocha if you're arriving/departing by train. Malasaña for a local, creative vibe. Salamanca for upscale and quiet.
Overall safety
Madrid is one of Europe's safest capital cities. The city center is busy and well-lit at night, and public transport feels safe even late. Emergency number: 112.
Pickpockets
The main risk for tourists. Watch your belongings around Sol, Gran Vía, Plaza Mayor, and on the metro during rush hour. Use a front pocket or crossbody bag. Be wary of people approaching with clipboards or "petitions."
Late-night safety
Madrid is famously a late-night city — restaurants are full at 11pm, bars at 1am. Most central neighborhoods feel safe late. Stick to busier streets in Lavapiés and around Atocha after dark.
Tip: Pharmacies (look for the green cross) are everywhere and can advise on minor health issues without needing a doctor. Most have English-speaking staff.
Omio
Book trains, buses, and flights across Europe. Handy for booking day trips to Toledo, Segovia, and other nearby cities directly from Madrid.
Citymapper or Google Maps
Both work well for Madrid metro/bus routing. Citymapper includes real-time departures and tells you which carriage to board.
Google Translate
Download the Spanish language pack for offline use. The camera mode is great for reading menus and signs.
Free Now / Cabify
Ride-hailing apps that work in Madrid. Useful for late-night trips or getting to/from the train station with luggage.
Spring packing: Madrid in March–May means warm days (18–25°C) and cooler evenings (8–12°C). Layers are essential.
Clothing
Light layers for daytime, a jacket for evenings. Madrid is a walking city — comfortable shoes are non-negotiable. A light scarf is handy for cooler evenings and visiting churches.
Sun protection
Even in spring, Madrid sun is strong (altitude 650m). Pack sunglasses and sunscreen. A hat is useful for day trips to Toledo/Segovia where shade is scarce.
Extras
A refillable water bottle (Madrid's tap water is excellent). A small daypack for day trips. A portable charger if you're using your phone for navigation all day.
Currency
Euro (€). Spain uses the euro, so no currency exchange needed if you're coming from another eurozone country.
Cards & cash
Credit and debit cards (Visa, Mastercard) are accepted almost everywhere, including small tapas bars and metro ticket machines. Contactless payments are standard. Keep some small cash (€20–30) for market stalls and very small bars.
ATMs
Widely available. Use bank ATMs (Santander, BBVA, CaixaBank) to avoid fees. Decline "dynamic currency conversion" — always pay in euros.
Tipping
Not expected in Spain. Locals might round up or leave a euro for good service. In upscale restaurants, 5–10% is generous. Never expected in bars or taxis.
Metro
13 lines covering the whole city. Single ticket €1.50. 10-trip pass €12.20. Runs 6am–1:30am.
Tourist card
Madrid Tourist Transport Pass: unlimited metro, bus, and Cercanías. Zone A from €8.40/day.
Taxi
Base fare €2.50 + €1.05/km. Uber and Cabify also available. Fixed airport fare €30.
Tip: Madrid's center is very walkable. From Atocha to Puerta del Sol is 20 minutes on foot, and you'll pass the Prado, Neptune fountain, and Plaza de Cibeles on the way.
