Eiffel Tower
The most visited paid monument in the world. Three-level iron lattice tower with restaurants, shops, and stunning city views. Best visited early morning or sunset.
📍 Champ de Mars | ⏱️ 1-2 hours | 💶 €11-17 entry
Louvre Museum
The world's largest art museum home to over 35,000 works. The Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo are highlights. Plan at least 2-3 hours, or visit specific galleries to avoid crowds.
📍 Right Bank | ⏱️ 2-4 hours | 💶 €17 entry
Musée d'Orsay
Housed in a converted Beaux-Arts railway station. World's finest Impressionist collection featuring Monet, Renoir, Van Gogh, and Degas. Less crowded than Louvre.
📍 Left Bank | ⏱️ 1.5-2.5 hours | 💶 €14 entry
Sacré-Cœur & Montmartre
White-domed basilica perched on Montmartre hill overlooking the city. Stunning Romano-Byzantine interior. Surrounding neighborhood offers street artists, bistros, and village-like charm.
📍 Montmartre, 18th arr. | ⏱️ 1-2 hours | 💶 Free basilica entry, €6 dome
Notre-Dame Cathedral
Iconic Gothic masterpiece currently undergoing restoration and reopening in 2024. Stunning rose windows and architectural details. External view worth the visit even during renovation.
📍 Île de la Cité | ⏱️ 45 min - 1 hour | 💶 Reopened with new entry details
Sainte-Chapelle
Stunning 13th-century Gothic chapel famous for 15 stained-glass windows depicting biblical scenes. Breathtaking interior light, especially at sunset. Often less crowded than major sites.
📍 Île de la Cité | ⏱️ 45 min | 💶 €11 entry
Panthéon
Neoclassical monument housing tombs of notable French figures like Voltaire and Rousseau. Dome interior with frescoes depicting Saint Genevieve. Stunning views from the top.
📍 Latin Quarter | ⏱️ 1-1.5 hours | 💶 €11 entry
Pro tip: Buy the Paris Museum Pass (€54 for 2 days, €69 for 4 days) for unlimited entry to 60+ sites including Louvre, Musée d'Orsay, and Sainte-Chapelle.
Seine River Cruise
Classic 1-hour cruise under the city's bridges with views of Eiffel Tower, Notre-Dame, Louvre, and riverside monuments. Perfect at sunset for romantic experience or golden-hour photography.
🚤 Multiple operators | 💶 €12-18 | ⏱️ 1 hour
French Cooking Classes
Learn traditional French cuisine from local chefs. Classes typically include market shopping, preparation, and a multi-course meal. Half-day and full-day options available in various neighborhoods.
👨‍🍳 Marais, Latin Quarter | 💶 €80-180 | ⏱️ 3-4 hours
Wine Tasting in Le Marais
Explore Le Marais' historic wine bars and contemporary wine shops. Guided tastings available or self-guided bar hopping. Learn about French wine regions while sampling local varieties.
🍷 Le Marais, 4th arr. | 💶 €15-30 per tasting | ⏱️ 2-3 hours
Flea Markets (Clignancourt)
Marché aux Puces de Saint-Ouen is one of Europe's largest flea markets. Browse vintage furniture, antiques, clothing, and curiosities across 100+ vendor stalls. Haggle is expected.
🏪 Saint-Ouen, north of Paris | 💶 Prices vary | ⏱️ 2-4 hours
Cycling Along Canal Saint-Martin
Rent a bike or Vélib' and pedal the scenic 4.5km canal path. Stop at cafés, picnic areas, and view charming lock bridges. Relaxing alternative to crowded tourist sites.
🚴 Canal Saint-Martin, 10th arr. | 💶 €2-5 bike rental | ⏱️ 1.5-2 hours
Moulin Rouge Cabaret Show
Iconic can-can dance hall established in 1889. Spectacular musical production with elaborate costumes, dancers, and live orchestra. Book in advance, especially for evening shows.
🎪 Pigalle, 18th arr. | 💶 €87-187 | ⏱️ 2 hours
Spring bonus: Flowering parks like Luxembourg Gardens and Tuileries are stunning during April-May. Free entry, perfect for picnics and people-watching.
Versailles Palace
Opulent former royal residence with stunning Hall of Mirrors, ornate gardens, and fountains. UNESCO World Heritage site. Plan a full day to explore palace, gardens, and Trianons.
🚊 Versailles | ⏱️ 30 min by RER | 💶 €18 entry
Giverny Gardens
Monet's former home and water lily pond gardens that inspired his masterpieces. Japanese bridge, weeping willows, and flower beds in full bloom during spring. Small village charm.
🚂 Vernon (Giverny) | ⏱️ 45 min by train | 💶 €11 entry
Reims & Champagne Region
UNESCO-listed Reims Cathedral and surrounding champagne houses. Tour prestigious champagne producers like Veuve Clicquot or Taittinger. Lunch in charming Reims town center.
🚄 Reims | ⏱️ 45 min by TGV | 💶 €10-25 tours
Fontainebleau Palace & Forest
Renaissance château surrounded by ancient forest. Famous for royal hunting grounds and art collections. Combine palace tour with hiking or picnicking in the stunning forest.
🚂 Fontainebleau | ⏱️ 40 min by train | 💶 €12 entry
Chartres Cathedral
Masterpiece of French Gothic architecture famous for stunning stained-glass windows and intricate rose windows. Located in charming Chartres town. Less crowded than Paris attractions.
🚂 Chartres | ⏱️ 1 hour by train | 💶 €15 entry
Spring tip: Giverny's gardens are at their peak in May. Book tickets online to skip queues. Consider visiting mid-week to avoid weekend crowds.
Croissants & Pastries
Paris croissants are incomparable. Buttery, flaky, authentic croissants au beurre from local boulangeries cost €1-2. Try pain au chocolat, pain aux amandes, and fruit tarts. Breakfast tradition.
☕ Local boulangeries everywhere | 💶 €1-3 | ⏱️ Quick morning stop
Bistro Culture
Classic French bistros serve traditional dishes: coq au vin, beef bourguignon, duck confit, sole meunière. Three-course menus with wine often better value than à la carte. Lunch menus typically cheaper than dinner.
🍷 Throughout Paris | 💶 €15-35 lunch, €25-50 dinner | ⏱️ 1.5-2 hours
Le Marais Food Scene
Historic Jewish quarter turned trendy neighborhood with diverse dining. Falafels, artisanal sandwiches, contemporary French fusion restaurants, concept cafés, and wine bars.
🥗 Le Marais, 4th arr. | 💶 €8-30 | ⏱️ Variable
Rue des Rosiers Falafel
Famous street in Le Marais serving Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisine. L'As du Fallafel and Chez Marianne are legendary spots. Casual, quick, authentic, and budget-friendly.
🌯 Le Marais, 4th arr. | 💶 €6-12 | ⏱️ 20-30 min
Market Street Dining
Rue Mouffetard (Latin Quarter) and Rue Cler (7th arr.) are lively market streets filled with fruit vendors, cheese stalls, charcuterie, wine shops, and casual eateries. Perfect for building a picnic.
🏪 Latin Quarter & 7th arr. | 💶 €2-15 per item | ⏱️ 1-2 hours browsing
Wine Bars (Caves à Vin)
Small neighborhood wine bars offering natural wines, by-the-glass pours, and charcuterie/cheese boards. Informal, social atmosphere. Perfect for evening aperitif or casual dinner.
🍷 Throughout Paris | 💶 €5-15 per glass, €20-30 boards | ⏱️ 1-2 hours
Parisian dining tip: Lunch is typically 12-2pm, dinner 7-10pm. Many restaurants close between services. Dress smartly for dinner reservations. Always greet servers with "Bonjour" or "Bonsoir".
Accommodation Costs
Budget: €50-80 per night (hostels, shared rooms). Mid-range: €100-200 per night (3-star hotels). Luxury: €250-600+ per night (4-5 star hotels). Spring season drives prices up 20-30%.
🏨 Prices vary by arr. | ⏱️ Book 1-2 months ahead for spring
Meal Costs
Budget meal: €8-12 (falafel, crêpes, sandwich). Bistro lunch: €15-25. Bistro dinner: €25-50. Fine dining: €60-150+. Breakfast croissants/coffee €3-5.
🍽️ Lunch menus better value | ⏱️ Budget €30-50/day food
Metro & Transport
T+ single ticket: €2.25. Carnet (10 tickets): €17.10 (€1.71 per trip). Navigo Découverte: €81.60 per week unlimited access to métro, bus, RER. Vélib' bikes: €5/day or €25/month.
🚇 16 métro lines | ⏱️ Buy carnet to save 25%
Museum & Attraction Prices
Eiffel Tower: €11-17. Louvre: €17. Musée d'Orsay: €14. Sainte-Chapelle: €11. Many museums free first Sunday of month.
🏛️ Budget €50-100 for museums | ⏱️ Check free hours
Paris Museum Pass
2-day pass: €54 (entry to 60+ sites including Louvre, Musée d'Orsay, Sainte-Chapelle, Versailles, Fontainebleau). 4-day pass: €69. Pays for itself after 3-4 major museums.
💳 Buy at airports or museums | ⏱️ Consecutive or not days
Budget strategy: Stay in outer arrondissements (11th, 13th) for cheaper hotels. Use Museum Pass. Lunch at bistros instead of dinner. Picnic from markets. Walk neighborhoods instead of tours.
Le Marais (4th arr.)
Historic Jewish quarter turned vibrant neighborhood. Narrow medieval streets, boutiques, galleries, vintage shops, wine bars, and diverse dining. Home to Place des Vosges arcaded square. Charming, walkable, central location.
🏘️ 4th arr. | 🚇 Métro: Saint-Paul, Bastille | 💰 Mid-to-upscale
Montmartre (18th arr.)
Hilltop bohemian village with Sacré-Cœur basilica, artist studios, street performers, white-draped streets. Historic cabarets like Moulin Rouge. More touristy but undeniably charming, especially lower slopes.
🏘️ 18th arr. | 🚇 Métro: Pigalle, Abbesses | 💰 Budget to mid-range
Saint-Germain-des-Prés (6th arr.)
Sophisticated Left Bank neighborhood famous for intellectual heritage, cafés (Café de Flore, Les Deux Magots), art galleries, and bookshops. Expensive but elegant. Boulevard Saint-Germain is central.
🏘️ 6th arr. | 🚇 Métro: Saint-Germain, Odéon | 💰 Upscale
Latin Quarter (5th arr.)
Student-filled neighborhood with Sorbonne University, Panthéon, Rue Mouffetard market street, and bookshop alleys. More budget-friendly despite central location. Lively nightlife and casual dining.
🏘️ 5th arr. | 🚇 Métro: Cluny-Sorbonne, Cardinal Lemoine | 💰 Budget
Canal Saint-Martin (10th arr.)
Emerging trendy area with 4.5km scenic canal, lock bridges, cafés along waterfront, contemporary restaurants, and arts venues. Less touristy than central areas. Perfect for cycling, picnics, evening walks.
🏘️ 10th arr. | 🚇 Métro: République, Jacques Bonsergent | 💰 Mid-range
Belleville (11th-20th arr.)
Multicultural neighborhood with street art, vintage shops, hole-in-the-wall eateries, and local neighborhood feel. Less polished than tourist areas but authentic and affordable. Great for street food and evening aperitifs.
🏘️ 11th-20th arr. | 🚇 Métro: Belleville, Parmentier | 💰 Budget
Neighborhood insight: Right Bank (1st-4th, 8th) more upscale and touristy. Left Bank (5th-7th) intellectual and bohemian. Outer arr. (10th, 11th, 13th, 20th) more local and affordable. Walk between neighborhoods for authentic discovery.
Budget Hostels
Dormitory beds €25-40, private rooms €50-80. Popular options: St Christopher's Inn (Belleville), Le Village Hostellerie (Marais area). Good for meeting travelers. Clean, social, but limited privacy.
🏢 Various arr. | 💶 €25-80/night | ⏱️ Book online
Mid-Range Hotels (3-star)
€100-200 per night. Comfortable rooms with private bathroom and basic amenities. Location matters: central arr. (1-6) more expensive; outer arr. (10-13, 20) cheaper. Spring season add 20-30% to prices.
🏨 Throughout Paris | 💶 €100-200/night | ⏱️ Book 2 months ahead spring
Luxury Hotels (4-5 star)
€250-600+ per night. High-end properties with premium service, dining, and location. Famous options: Le Meurice, Ritz, Peninsula. Often in 1st, 6th, 8th arr. Spring significantly raises rates.
🏰 Prime locations | 💶 €250-600+/night | ⏱️ Book well ahead
Airbnb & Vacation Rentals
Studio apartments €60-120, one-bedroom €80-200. Offers kitchen access and local living experience. Reading reviews essential. Note: Paris has strict short-term rental regulations; verify legitimacy.
🏠 Throughout Paris | 💶 €60-200/night | ⏱️ Filter by location
Location Strategy
Central arr. (1-6) pricier but walkable. Save money in 10th, 11th, 13th, 20th: métro-connected, local feel, better value. Avoid 8th, 16th unless budget allows—expensive, less character.
💡 Outer arr. closer to République, Gare du Nord, Belleville | 🚇 Good métro access
Spring booking tip: Easter holiday (April) drives occupancy high. Book 6-8 weeks ahead for decent rates. Weekday rates often 10-20% cheaper than weekends.
Pickpocket Awareness
Pickpocketing is common in tourist hotspots: Eiffel Tower lines, métro during rush hour, crowded train stations. Never leave bag unattended. Wear backpack in front on crowded métro. Keep valuables in inside pockets.
⚠️ Métro, tourist sites | 💡 Use money belt, crossbody bag
Scam Awareness
Common scams: fake bracelet sellers (resist firmly), petition scammers asking you to sign then demand donations, fake police checking documents. Don't engage, walk away politely. Verify restaurants before entering.
⚠️ Tourist areas, parks | 💡 Ignore solicitors, trust instinct
General Safety
Paris is generally safe. Central and tourist areas well-policed. Outer arr. 13th, 19th, 20th have rougher neighborhoods—avoid late night. Woman travelers generally safe; use common sense alone at night. Avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
✅ Generally safe | 🚫 Avoid deserted streets late night
Emergency Numbers
Police: 17 | Fire: 18 | Ambulance: 15 | General emergency: 112. Learn basic French phrases for help. Most police speak some English. Carry a copy of passport separately from original.
📞 Police, fire, ambulance numbers | 💡 Have phone with local SIM
Safety summary: Paris is safe for tourists who use basic precautions. Stay aware in crowded tourist spots and on métro. Keep valuables secure. Avoid late-night solo travel in outer arr. Trust your instinct.

Layers & Clothing
Spring weather unpredictable: 10-18°C (50-64°F). Pack light sweaters, cardigans, light jacket. Layers allow flexibility. Comfortable walking shoes (cobblestones hard on feet). One dressier outfit for restaurants/shows.
👕 Layers + light jacket | 👟 Comfortable walking shoes
Weather Essentials
Umbrella or lightweight rain jacket essential—spring showers frequent. Sunscreen for longer sightseeing days. Sunglasses for bright spring days. Hat optional but useful for sun and rain protection.
☔ Compact umbrella | 🧴 SPF 30+ sunscreen
Footwear
Comfortable walking shoes absolutely essential—expect 10,000+ steps daily exploring neighborhoods. Cobblestones and uneven pavement hard on feet. Break in shoes before trip. Backup pair helpful.
👟 Quality walking shoes | 💡 Worn-in before arrival
Dressy Outfit
Parisians dress well, especially for dinner. Pack one nicer outfit for restaurants, theaters, or shows. Avoid athletic wear for evening dining. Comfortable dressier shoes or flats helpful.
👗 Neat casual dress + flats or heels | 💡 Respectful appearance
Electronics & Adapters
France uses Type E/F plugs (European standard). Universal adapter required. Bring portable charger—sightseeing drains phones. Phone with data plan or local SIM for navigation and transit apps.
🔌 Type E/F adapter | 🔋 Power bank
Packing strategy: Pack light—you'll buy souvenirs. Carry reusable water bottle (fountains throughout Paris). Day pack/crossbody bag for sightseeing. Leave oversized luggage at accommodation.
Currency: Euro (€)
France uses the Euro (EUR). Current exchange rates ~€1 = $1.10 USD. ATMs widely available for cash withdrawal. Most transactions card-based. No need to carry large amounts of cash.
💱 €1 ≈ $1.10 USD | 🏧 ATMs everywhere
Card Payments
Debit and credit cards (Visa, Mastercard, Amex) widely accepted. Contactless payments very common. Some small vendors (tiny cafés, street vendors) cash-only. Chip-and-PIN or contactless standard; signatures rarely requested.
💳 Visa/Mastercard/Amex | 📱 Contactless payments
ATM & Cash Withdrawals
ATMs (Distributeurs) on every major street. Most major cards work (Visa, Mastercard). Withdraw €200-300 in chunks to minimize bank fees. Notify your bank of travel dates to avoid card blocks.
🏧 Widespread ATMs | ☎️ Notify bank before travel
Tipping Culture
Tipping not mandatory. Service included in restaurant bills. Rounding up (€2 on €18 bill) or 5-10% appreciated but optional. Tip more for exceptional service. Café staff rarely tipped. Tour guides/hotel staff: €2-5 appreciated.
💡 Service included | 💶 Round up or 5-10% optional
Exchange Rates
Airport exchanges charge high fees. Use ATMs for best rates. Credit cards offer decent exchange rates with possible small fee. Avoid currency exchange booths—overcharged. Check bank rates before traveling.
💱 ATMs best rates | ✅ Credit card acceptable | ❌ Avoid exchanges
Money strategy: Use debit card at ATMs for cash. Pay by card when possible. Keep €50-100 cash for small vendors. Inform bank before travel. Set daily withdrawal limit if ATM offers option.
Paris Métro
16-line subway system covering entire city. Trains run 5:30am-1:15am (2:15am Fri-Sat). Most efficient transport for longer distances. Color-coded lines make navigation intuitive. Métro map available at stations or in apps.
🚇 16 lines | ⏱️ Every 2-5 min frequency | 💶 €2.25 single ticket
T+ Tickets & Carnet
Single ticket valid for 1 journey (any distance) on métro, bus, or RER. Buy individual tickets €2.25 or carnet (10 tickets) €17.10 for 24% savings. Tickets valid unlimited time once inserted.
🎫 €2.25 single | €17.10 carnet (10) | ⏱️ Buy at stations
Navigo Découverte Pass
Weekly unlimited pass: €81.60. Valid Monday-Sunday on all métro, bus, and RER within Île-de-France. Good for longer stays (1+ week). Requires photo ID and is personal to cardholder.
💳 €81.60/week | ✅ Unlimited all transport | 📷 Requires photo
Buses
Extensive bus network covering areas métro doesn't. Uses same T+ tickets. Slower than métro but better for sightseeing. Night buses (N lines) run after métro closes. Less crowded than métro at most times.
🚌 Same T+ tickets | ⏱️ Variable frequency | 🌙 Night buses available
RER Regional Trains
Faster regional trains connecting central Paris to outer areas and airports. 5 lines (A-E). Used for day trips to Versailles, Giverny, and airports. Same T+ tickets valid but watch zone pricing for longer trips.
🚊 5 lines A-E | ⏱️ Every 10-15 min | 🎫 T+ or zone tickets
Vélib' Bikes
Self-service bike-sharing system with 1,400+ stations across Paris. Day pass €5, 7-day €25. First 30 min free each trip. Excellent for Canal Saint-Martin, parks, and leisurely exploration. Helmets rare but recommended.
🚴 €5/day or €25/week | 🗺️ 1,400+ stations | ⏱️ First 30 min free
Taxis & Ride-Share
Taxis licensed, meter-required. Typical metro ride €8-15. Uber and Bolt available but often more expensive than métro. Taxis useful late night when métro closed or heavy luggage. Airport taxis fixed-rate ~€35.
🚕 Métro more economical | 📱 Uber/Bolt available | ✅ Taxis safe
Walking
Many attractions within walking distance. Central arr. (1-6) walkable between areas. Great for neighborhood discovery and soaking in Parisian street life. Wear comfortable shoes—cobblestones tiring after hours.
👟 Best for short distances | 🗺️ Use Google Maps | ⏱️ Allow extra time
Transport strategy: Buy carnet for flexibility without daily pass commitment. Use métro for longer distances, walk for neighborhoods. Rent Vélib' for scenic routes. Save day trips for RER/trains. Avoid taxis unless necessary—métro/bus sufficient and cheaper.
